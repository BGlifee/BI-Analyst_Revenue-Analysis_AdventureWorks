## 🔍 Executive Summary
Revenue is primarily driven by high-value bike purchases, with strong seasonal demand and significant influence from AOV rather than order volume.

---

## 🔶 1. Revenue Structure

| Category | Findings | Insight | Recommendation |
|----------|--------|--------|----------------|
| Product & Customer Structure | Bike category generates **$36.07M (~84%)** of total revenue ($43.11M). Customer base is **~10.85K**, with a high **AOV of $3.12K**. | Revenue is highly concentrated in a single product category and driven by **high-value purchases rather than large customer volume**, indicating both strength and concentration risk. | - Diversify revenue by promoting **non-bike categories (Accessories, Apparel)** through bundling.<br>- Focus on **premium bike segments** to maintain high AOV.<br>- Identify and retain **high-value customers (top spenders)** via loyalty programs. |

---

## 🔷 2. Time Dimension (Seasonality)

| Category | Findings | Insight | Recommendation |
|----------|--------|--------|----------------|
| Seasonal Demand | Revenue peaks in **Q2 (~$10M+) and Q3 (~$12M+)**, with noticeable spikes in **July and October**. | Strong **seasonality pattern** exists, with demand concentrated in mid-to-late year periods. Spikes suggest **promotion-driven or peak-season demand effects**. | - Align **inventory planning** with Q2–Q3 demand peaks to avoid stockouts.<br>- Schedule **major promotions and campaigns in July and October**.<br>- Use off-season (Q1, early Q4) for **discount strategies or demand stimulation**. |

---

## 🔸 3. Revenue Drivers (Volume vs Value)

| Category | Findings | Insight | Recommendation |
|----------|--------|--------|----------------|
| AOV vs Order Volume | Monthly revenue reaches **~$5M peak**, while order volume varies (~2K–6K). Some months (e.g., **September**) maintain high revenue despite lower order volume. | Revenue is not purely volume-driven; **AOV plays a critical role**. High revenue periods can be driven by **premium product mix or pricing strategy**, not just more orders. | - Increase AOV via **upselling and product bundling strategies**.<br>- Promote **high-margin / premium products** during key periods.<br>- Segment customers and target **high-spending behavior** instead of only increasing order count. |

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways
- Revenue is heavily dependent on the **Bike category**, creating both strong performance and potential concentration risk.  
- **High AOV ($3.12K)** indicates a premium purchasing pattern rather than high transaction frequency.  
- Clear **seasonality (Q2–Q3 peaks)** highlights the importance of timing in revenue generation.  
- Revenue is often driven more by **customer value (AOV)** than by sheer order volume.
